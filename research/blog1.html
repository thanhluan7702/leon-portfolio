Apply a machine learning model to perform data imputation on 1D time series data

1. General Introduction

- Time series data plays an undeniable role in our daily life, it is recorded continiously over-time. This data is often captured in fixed time units such as years, months, days, hours, ... Examples of such data include financial information like stock price or business data like sales figure.

- Handling missing data is a critical task in time series analysis, as it can significantly impact the performance of predictive models. Imputation methods for one-dimensional (1D) time series data must consider the temporal correlation between observations to preserve the underlying patterns.

2. Methodology

- Division: To address missing data, we categorize it into three cases for efficient processing. First, if gaps occur at the beginning or end of the data sequence, defined as missing data of size T within the first or last 3*T sections, we use the adjacent data for model training and prediction. If this condition isnâ€™t met, we identify gaps in the middle of the sequence and split the data into two parts-before and after the gap-to train the model and predict values. This flexible approach ensures accurate and reliable estimations.

- Data transformation: transform the data from a univariate format to a multivariate (T+1)-dimensional. This transformation enables leveraging preceding data to impute the necessary subsequent data effectively.

- Algorithm: Arima, Regression model (Decision Tree, SVM, ...)

- Evaluation metrics: Similarity, MAE, RMSE, FB (Forecast bias), FSD (Fraction of Standard Deviation). Among them, FB and FSD are overall evaluation metrics, while the other three metrics evaluate individual instances.

3. Experiments

In this survey, I do not prioritize any specific evaluation metric, as this depends on the range and values of the research data. However, an overall assessment in the three cases shows that ARIMA performs better than ML, but it would be even better with a combination of the two methods, where Layer 1 is ARIMA and Layer 2 is the ML model (based on the residuals of ARIMA).
